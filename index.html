Cup Strength (SOF)

Measures how stacked a cup's lobby is compared to a theoretical maximum field.

How it's calculated

  • All rated players are normalized to a 0–2000 scale, where the #1 rated player = 2000.
  • From each cup, the top 10 finishers by normalized rating are selected.
  • If fewer than 10 rated players, remaining slots are padded with the lowest rated player in the pool.
SOF = average(top 10) / 1850 × 100

Why top 10?

Lobby sizes vary wildly (5 to 60+). Using the top 10 makes cups comparable regardless of how many casuals show up. A 58-player cup and a 35-player cup with the same elite players get the same SOF.

Why normalize?

As ELO ratings inflate over time, raw values would make older cups look weaker. Normalization scales everyone relative to the current best, keeping the metric fair across all cups.

Reference

99%+All-time stacked. Big 3 + top 10 present. Only 6 cups ever hit this.
97–93%Strong cup. Some top players missing, top 20 fill the gaps.
90–80%Weak cup. Some top players present, top 40 players fill the top 10.

Weighted ELO — Changelog

18.02.26
  • Position-based K multiplier — top 8% of finishers earn 3× the baseline points, top 15% earn 2×, top 25% earn 1.3×, bottom 50% earn 0.5×. Rewards strong finishes more than just participating.
  • Lobby quality weighting — wins against high-rated opponents in competitive lobbies are worth proportionally more. Addresses the Big 3 effect (justMaki / Kernkob / ZOMAN winning ~64% of cups).
  • Zero-sum — ELO gained by winners exactly equals ELO lost by losers each cup.
  • Decay improved — players keep their full rating for up to 3 missed cups (grace period), then decay at 0.5% per missed cup. Replaces old 1%/cup with no grace, which was too harsh for returning high-rated players.
  • Weekly arrows — now show raw ELO earned/lost, not the decayed active rating change.
